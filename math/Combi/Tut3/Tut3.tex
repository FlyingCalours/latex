\paragraph{Q10} Generate the 3-permutations of $\{1,2,3,4,5\}$.

\medskip \noindent \textit{Solution.} 

\noindent
Generating the 3-combinations, we have:
\[123,\quad124,\quad125,\quad134,\quad135,\quad145,\quad234,\quad235,\quad245,\quad345.\]

\noindent
Note that we generated 10 3-combinations, and $\displaystyle{{5 \choose 3}} = 10.$

\bigskip
\noindent
Now, permutating through each of the 10 3-combinations, we have:
\[
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|c|}
    123 & 124 & 125 & 134 & 135 & 145 & 234 & 235 & 245 & 345 \\
    132 & 142 & 152 & 143 & 153 & 154 & 243 & 253 & 254 & 354 \\
    312 & 412 & 512 & 413 & 513 & 514 & 423 & 523 & 524 & 534 \\
    321 & 421 & 521 & 431 & 531 & 541 & 432 & 532 & 542 & 543 \\
    231 & 241 & 251 & 341 & 351 & 451 & 342 & 352 & 452 & 453 \\
    213 & 214 & 215 & 314 & 315 & 415 & 324 & 325 & 425 & 435 \\
\end{array}
\]

\noindent
In total, we have $10 \times 6 = 60$ 3-permutations. (note that $P(5,3) = 5 \times 4 \times 3 = 60$)



\hfill $\square$
